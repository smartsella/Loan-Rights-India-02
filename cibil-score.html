CIBIL Score Calculation Guide

Understand how your credit score is calculated, what factors influence it, and how to improve your CIBIL score for better loan opportunities.

Calculation Process Improvement Tips

Understanding CIBIL Score

A CIBIL score is a three-digit numeric summary of your credit history, representing your creditworthiness. This score ranges from 300 to 900, with higher scores indicating better credit health.

Lenders use your CIBIL score to evaluate your credit risk and determine your eligibility for loans and credit cards. A good score can help you secure loans at favorable interest rates and terms.

300 (Poor) 600 (Fair) 750 (Good) 900 (Excellent)
Did You Know? Your CIBIL score is based on your credit history from the past 36 months (3 years) of your credit behavior.

CIBIL Score Calculation Process

How your credit score is calculated based on various factors

1

Payment History (35%)

Your payment history is the most significant factor in your CIBIL score calculation. It reflects how consistently you've made payments on your credit accounts.

What's Considered:
  • Timeliness of EMI payments
  • Credit card bill payments
  • Any missed or delayed payments
  • Defaults or settlements
  • How recent any negative items are
2

Credit Utilization (30%)

This measures how much of your available credit you're using. It's calculated for each card and across all cards.

What's Considered:
  • Ratio of current balance to credit limit
  • Individual card utilization
  • Overall credit utilization across all cards
  • Trends in utilization over time
Tip: Keep your credit utilization below 30% of your available limit for the best score impact.
3

Credit Mix & Duration (25%)

This factor considers the types of credit you have and how long you've had them.

What's Considered:
  • Types of credit (secured vs unsecured)
  • Diversity of credit accounts
  • Age of oldest credit account
  • Average age of all accounts
  • How long since accounts were opened
4

Other Factors (10%)

This includes recent credit behavior and inquiries made by lenders.

What's Considered:
  • Number of recent credit inquiries
  • New credit accounts opened
  • Recent credit behavior patterns
  • Time since most recent account opening
Warning: Too many hard inquiries in a short period can negatively impact your score as it may indicate credit hunger.

Improving Your CIBIL Score

Practical steps to boost your credit score over time

Short-Term Strategies (1-3 months)

Pay Down Credit Card Balances

Reduce your credit utilization ratio by paying down outstanding balances, especially on credit cards.

Avoid New Credit Applications

Minimize hard inquiries by not applying for new credit unless absolutely necessary.

Set Up Payment Reminders

Ensure you never miss a payment deadline by setting up alerts or auto-pay options.

Long-Term Strategies (6+ months)

Maintain a Diverse Credit Mix

Responsibly manage different types of credit (secured and unsecured) to show you can handle various credit products.

Keep Old Accounts Open

Maintain your oldest credit accounts to preserve your credit history length.

Review Your Credit Report Regularly

Check your credit report for errors and dispute any inaccuracies that might be hurting your score.

What to Avoid

  • Missing payment due dates
  • Utilizing more than 30% of your credit limit
  • Applying for multiple credit products simultaneously
  • Closing old credit accounts
  • Defaulting on loans or settling debts
  • Ignoring errors in your credit report

Understanding Your Credit Report

How to read and interpret your CIBIL report

Components of a CIBIL Report

Your CIBIL report contains several sections that provide a comprehensive view of your credit history:

Section Description Importance
Personal Information Your name, date of birth, gender, and identification numbers Ensures your identity is correctly matched
Contact Information Your addresses and phone numbers Helps lenders verify your residence history
Employment Information Your income and employer details Provides context for your repayment capacity
Account Information Detailed history of all credit accounts Core component that determines your score
Enquiry Information Record of all lenders who have accessed your report Shows how often you've applied for credit
You are entitled to one free credit report per year from each credit bureau in India. Regularly reviewing your report helps you identify errors and detect potential identity theft.
