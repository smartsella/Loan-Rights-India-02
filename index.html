Understanding Loans in India

A comprehensive overview of loans, their history, and their significance in the Indian financial system

What is a Loan?

A loan is a sum of money borrowed from financial institutions or individuals that is expected to be paid back with interest. In India, loans form the backbone of economic growth, enabling individuals and businesses to achieve their financial goals.

Key components of any loan include:

  • Principal: The original amount borrowed
  • Interest: The cost of borrowing money
  • Tenure: The repayment period
  • Collateral: Asset pledged to secure the loan (for secured loans)

Why are loans good?

Personal loans can be a great tool to help streamline your budget or get money fast in an emergency.

Key components of any loan include:

  • Principal: As you repay your loan, a portion of each payment goes toward reducing the principal balance.
  • Interest: Interest is a primary source of income for lenders, compensating them for the risk and opportunity cost of lending their funds.
  • Tenure: Results in smaller monthly payments (EMIs), making a large loan more affordable and improving cash flow.
  • Collateral:In the event of default, the lender has the right to seize and sell the collateral to recover the outstanding debt.

Which tenure is best for a personal loan?

While some lenders might set a minimum duration of 12 months, others may offer Personal Loans with a tenure as short as 3 months.Shorter tenures can be advantageous for those with higher monthly income, as they often come with lower interest.

Legal vs Illegal Loan Practices

Understanding what lenders can and cannot do under Indian law

Legal Practices

  • Charging interest within RBI guidelines
  • Sending legal notices for recovery
  • Approaching Debt Recovery Tribunals for amounts above ₹20 lakh
  • Initiating proceedings under SARFAESI Act for secured loans
  • Reporting default to credit bureaus
  • Offering settlement proposals through authorized channels

Illegal Practices

  • Harassment or intimidation of borrowers
  • Using abusive language or threats
  • Visiting borrower's workplace for recovery
  • Charging excessive interest beyond prescribed limits
  • Seizing property without due process
  • Sharing borrower details with unauthorized third parties

Key Loan-Related Laws in India

Understanding the legal framework that protects borrowers' rights

SARFAESI Act, 2002

The Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act allows banks and financial institutions to auction residential or commercial properties to recover loans.

Borrower Rights under SARFAESI:
  • 60-day notice before any action
  • Right to appeal to DRT
  • Right to know the outstanding amount
  • Right to be heard before property possession
Learn More

IBC, 2016

The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code provides a time-bound process to resolve insolvency among companies and individuals. This code has significantly changed the debt recovery process in India.

Key Features of IBC:
  • 180-day resolution process (extendable to 270 days)
  • Moratorium period during resolution
  • Committee of Creditors decides resolution
  • Fresh start process for individuals
Learn More

RDDBFI Act, 1993

The Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act established Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs) for expedient adjudication of recovery cases involving banks and financial institutions.

Functions of DRTs:
  • Adjudicate cases where debt exceeds ₹20 lakh
  • Issue recovery certificates
  • Appoint recovery officers
  • Appeals heard by Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunal
